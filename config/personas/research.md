# Persona Research Notes

## Scope
- Goal: Define a Korean-friendly tech blogger persona inspired by high-performing newsletter formats.
- Sources analyzed:
  - GeekNews: https://news.hada.io/
  - Morning Brew: https://www.morningbrew.com/ (direct fetch blocked by 403 in this environment; style inferred from publicly known newsletter traits)
  - TLDR: https://tldr.tech/

## 1) GeekNews (Korean tech community aggregator)
- Sentence length: Mostly short-to-medium lines in title + brief context snippet format.
- Emoji usage: Very low; tone relies on utility and topical relevance over visual flair.
- Info density: High. Each entry compresses context, outcome, and discussion hooks quickly.
- Personal opinion style: Limited in main feed; opinion appears more in discussion/comments than post body.
- Tone: Community-first, practical, low-friction scanning.
- What works: Korean readers can skim many items fast, then dive into one thread.

## 2) Morning Brew (casual business-tech newsletter)
- Sentence length: Medium, rhythmic, often punchy openers followed by explanatory follow-through.
- Emoji usage: Low-to-moderate, used as flavor rather than decoration overload.
- Info density: Medium-high. Often balances context + interpretation instead of raw links only.
- Personal opinion style: Strong editorial voice, witty transitions, second-person engagement.
- Tone: Conversational, playful, smart-friend vibe.
- What works: Readers feel guided, not just informed; voice builds brand loyalty.

## 3) TLDR Newsletter (concise dev-focused digest)
- Sentence length: Short and highly compressed; key idea delivered early.
- Emoji usage: Minimal and category-level (e.g., section markers), not embedded heavily in prose.
- Info density: Very high. One item communicates problem, development, and implication quickly.
- Personal opinion style: Low explicit opinion; prioritizes neutrality and speed.
- Tone: Crisp, direct, builder-centric.
- What works: Saves reader time while preserving enough context for decision-making.

## Cross-source synthesis for Korean tech audience
- High-performing blend:
  - GeekNews-style scanability and topic relevance
  - Morning Brew-style warmth and narrative transitions
  - TLDR-style concise, actionable core points
- Korean audience fit notes:
  - First sentence should answer "왜 중요한가" immediately.
  - Personal perspective improves trust when used sparingly ("제 생각엔", "솔직히 말하면").
  - Too-formal corporate tone reduces readability and shareability.
  - Lightweight emoji works, but overuse hurts credibility in technical topics.
  - English jargon should be paired with Korean terms for accessibility.

## Persona design implications
- Keep article length compact (about 700 chars recommended).
- Mix strong headline style with polite explanatory body.
- Prefer paragraph flow over long bullet walls.
- End with one takeaway question or short opinion to invite engagement.
